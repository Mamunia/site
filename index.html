I am currently studying M.Sc. in Glass & Ceramic Engineering at Bangladesh University of Engineering & Technology (BUET) and will be graduating in October 2019. I received my B.Sc in Materials & Metallurgical Engineering from the same university in February, 2017. My research interests lie at the intersection of Materials Science & the Physics of Condensed Matter Theory.

My M.Sc. thesis focuses on the role of oxygen vacancies in oxide based Dilute Magnetic Semiconductors (DMS) which is one of the most promising candidates for spintronics device. The innovation of a stable room temperature DMS is mainly limited by the controversial debate about the origin of ferromagnetism in these materials. DMS materials are actually non – magnetic materials containing very small amounts of magnetic ions as dopants. Due to these magnetic dopants, the non – magnetic materials show dilute ferromagnetism. But the basic difference between DMS and other ferromagnetic materials is DMS’s half metallic behavior which alone controls the spin polarized behavior in these materials. If this half-metallic behavior can be made stable at room temperature, then these DMS materials will bring a paradigm shift in technology by integrated in logic devices, spin polarized light emitting diodes, non – volatile memory storages, spin field effect transistors etc.
